Chapter One

Am I Alive?

In 2302, Asha enters the abandoned ruins of NOVALIS and discovers a frozen human child carrying the same consciousness as her own.

The encounter raises the central question of the first chapter: when two versions of one consciousness exist, which one is truly alive?

Original music, 3D environments, animation and post-production completed.
Read the full synopsis

“Am I Alive?” opens the saga in 2302, long after the Last War. Asha, a Nomira from the ExNova-1 reconnaissance squad, enters the abandoned ruins of NOVALIS. While the rest of her team moves towards a mysterious source of infinite energy, she follows a trail of powered cables beneath an old bridge.

There, hidden inside a forgotten tunnel, she discovers a cryogenic chamber containing the last human child. What Asha does not yet fully understand is that the child is also her. The child's consciousness was duplicated and transferred into Asha. While the original human body and mind remained frozen in time, Asha continued to live, learn and grow through an entire lifetime.

Foxako is a music-and-visual project created in French-speaking Switzerland by Bartekola and Keynok.

Bartekola develops the music, narrative direction and 3D animation. Keynok develops the illustrated identity and 2D visual language.

The world

NOVALIS

NOVALIS is the narrative foundation connecting every Foxako release: a five-arc science-fiction saga. The audience enters through its final era first, discovering the consequences before the causes, so the mystery leads. Each new release then rewinds to an earlier point and moves forward, answering the first chapter's questions one piece at a time.

Why start at the end?

Instead of explaining everything, the first chapter presents the consequences before the causes: an abandoned city, conscious machines inheriting human memories and one impossible encounter hidden beneath the ruins.

Rather than continue from there, the next chapters rewind to an earlier era and move forward through the timeline. Only much later does the story reach that final era again, and reveal what happens after the ending left in suspense.

NOVALIS uses a stylised low-poly language inspired by early 3D games, Y2K culture and retrofuturist science fiction. The objective is not photorealism: simplified forms leave more space for composition, light, atmosphere and emotion.
